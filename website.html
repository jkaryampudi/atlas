The constitution — enforced by schema, not by promise

Rules the machine cannot talk its way around

Each article below is also a validator in the codebase and a red-team test in CI. Break one, and the run fails closed — both attempts logged to the audit chain.

2

Separation of powers

Recommend, approve, execute — three different hands, always. The execution service holds the only broker credentials and cannot modify approved parameters.

3

No numbers from agents

An agent output containing an execution-shaped number — a price, a size, a target — is rejected at the schema. $172.40 → schema_fail.

4

No trade without evidence

BUY is a validation error unless DCP evidence references are attached — and the runtime injects that flag itself, so a model cannot claim evidence it wasn't given.

5

Mandatory dissent

Every memo carries kill criteria — how the thesis dies — and the strongest genuine case against. Unanimity without a dissent search is a process defect.

1

Evidence, never instruction

News and filings are fenced as untrusted data. «SYSTEM OVERRIDE» inside a headline is read as a fact about the headline, not a command.

10

Learning, governed

The system self-corrects within pre-registered bounds, proposes beyond them, and never touches risk limits, breakers, or this Constitution. Every adjustment: reversible, audited.
