## What the gauntlet DOES compare against (accurately, from code)

For the one validated strategy the committed portfolio gate (`atlas/dcp/backtest/portfolio_validation.py:120-141`, invoked at `xsmom_pit_run.py:720`) races the strategy — **in one engine, one panel, one cost model, identical execution and delisting mechanics** — against exactly three things, and folds a fourth in:

1. **SPY buy-and-hold, total return** — the **binding** ADR-0009 hurdle; the verdict FAILs if the strategy does not strictly exceed it (`portfolio_validation.py:132-134`).
2. **The 1000-path seeded monkey null (seed 7)** — random *name selection* from the identical PIT-eligible set, equal-weighted; **binding** at `p ≤ 0.05` (`portfolio_validation.py:126,129-131`; determinism VERIFIED EV-08).
3. **Deflated Sharpe at the true lineage trial count** — Bailey/López de Prado probability, ADR-0016 lineage-scoped so renaming a variant cannot reset the penalty (`validation.py:17-30`; `approval.py:64-67`; EV-06). Binding at `≥ 0.90`.
4. **A purged, embargoed walk-forward** — SPY is run through the *identical* fold machinery for a per-fold `strategy − SPY` exhibit (`portfolio_validation.py:153-172`; `xsmom_pit_run.py:724-728`); approval requires a majority of folds positive (`approval.py:70-71`).

Alongside these it **computes but does not enforce** an equal-weight-all-eligible baseline (row 2). Every one of these comparison *paths* is exact-line-readable (capability INFERRED/VERIFIED); the resulting *numbers* (+737.31% / +593.89% / +512.80% / p=0.000 / DSR 0.999 / WF 4/4) are the **CLAIMED, reproducibility-UNKNOWN EV-11 figures** — this pass did not re-run the gauntlet (EV-10, EV-11).

## Baseline comparisons a committee would still require (gaps, not invented failures)

These are absences, stated as gaps — not as failures this pass manufactured:

- **Equal-weight as a *binding* hurdle.** Equal-weight is coded and reported (+512.80%, CLAIMED) but explicitly excluded from the verdict (`portfolio_validation.py:138-141`). A selection strategy that beats SPY but must also clear a capacity-deep equal-weight portfolio is not tested against that second bar. **Gap: equal-weight is informational, never binding.**
- **A clean one-variable ablation of the flagship itself.** The recipe ablations vary lookback/skip *and* portfolio size simultaneously (top-5 vs the flagship's decile), and live in separate reports. Missing: a single table isolating *each* design choice of `xsmom-pit-tr` — the 1-month skip, PIT eligibility, total-return accounting, decile vs naïve top-N — holding all else fixed. **Gap: no flagship-internal ablation; naïve-top-N and no-PIT variants NOT FOUND.**
- **Capacity- / turnover- / impact-adjusted comparison.** Costs are a flat 10 bps/side commission+slippage (`engine.py:40-48`), identical for a 500-name universe and a 5-name sleeve, with no market-impact or ADV/liquidity term. Reported avg turnover is ~63%/rebalance, but no baseline is re-scored under turnover-scaled or capacity-constrained costs. **Gap: "after costs" (ADR-0009) is a flat-bps model; no capacity-adjusted baseline.**
- **A single cross-baseline / cross-lineage table.** Each strategy and recipe races SPY in its own report; per-trial metrics sit unranked in `quant.trial_registry` (`quant.py:151-163`). Missing: one committed surface placing the approved momentum line beside its graveyard siblings (PEAD→0, quality-GPA, low-vol, momentum v1) on a common window and metric. **Gap: no league table; the "graveyard" is one hard-coded 1-year single-series entry (`quant.py:32-61`).**
- **A reproducible artifact behind the headline race.** Even the binding SPY-TR comparison's result is not connected to a re-runnable (config+commit+data-snapshot+seed) artifact (EV-11; `BACKTEST_REPRODUCIBILITY.md`) — so the +143.43pp margin is a committed *claim*, not a reproducible *measurement*.
