## 2. Assumptions most likely to be wrong (ranked)

Ranked by (arbitrariness × portfolio impact). Every item here is **ARBITRARY (policy choice)** or an
unvalidated modeling assumption, and each is high-leverage.

1. **QA-05 — the DSR trial count (n), and the grandfathered flagship.** This is the most damaging.
   EV-06 *executed* the arithmetic: the single approved live strategy clears the 0.90 gate **only at
   n_trials=1 (DSR≈0.999)** and **fails at its own lineage count (n≈23, DSR≈0.853)**. ADR-0016 admits
   it "would not clear the bar" but rules "this run stands." The system's one deployed edge is
   below its own current statistical bar; only a governance grandfather keeps it live.

2. **QA-02 / QA-01 — the flat 10 bps/side cost model with no impact/spread/ADV term.** Every
   performance number (EV-11) is net of exactly this and nothing more. The code itself concedes
   frictions "beyond 10 bps/side … NOT modeled" and "no liquidity screen behind them." A top-decile,
   5-name momentum book at A$100k is precisely where impact-vs-ADV bites, and it is structurally
   absent. Under-costing directly inflates the beat-SPY margin (QA-39) and the Sharpe feeding QA-05.

3. **QA-04 — the normal-returns assumption inside the DSR.** The central statistical gate is computed
   under Gaussianity. Real returns are fat-tailed and left-skewed, which makes the Gaussian DSR
   **overstate** skill probability — i.e. the 0.90 gate is more permissive than it looks, compounding
   QA-05. Never tested against any strategy's actual return distribution in-repo.

4. **QA-17 / QA-18 — the 40 % single-strategy momentum sleeve, sized at the 8 % L1 cap.** A
   concentrated, long-only, top-5 directional bet. ADR-0017 signs in ink for ≈−16pp of NAV in a
   momentum crash *before* the demotion band trips, plus structural cash drag. Chosen "from a menu",
   not optimised.

5. **QA-20 — demotion bands calibrated to the strategy's own in-sample worst case (−36.9 %→−40 %).**
   A band set from the same record it polices only fires *after* the book has already matched its
   historical worst loss; anything beyond the 2012-2026 sample is unbanded. Marked `provisional`, and
   the promised *derived* contract (and its CUSUM leg, QA-37) is absent for the live strategy.

6. **QA-21 / QA-26 / QA-23 / QA-33 — the core risk-limit levels (8 % name, 1 % risk/trade, 25 %
   sector, −5/−10/−15 % breakers).** All round-number policy choices with no in-repo calibration.
   Individually defensible as conservative heuristics; collectively they are the *entire* quantitative
   floor under a capital-preservation mandate, and none is derived from measured volatility or
   tail statistics.

7. **QA-03 / QA-06 — the gate levels themselves (DSR 0.90, p 0.05).** "At agreed significance" with
   no derivation. 0.90 is neither the conventional 0.95 nor 0.975; the pass/fail of the whole program
   hinges on a chosen constant.
