# Board Memo — Atlas AI Capital, Phase-5 Review

**To:** Jay (Principal) · **From:** Advisory Board Chair · **Re:** Seven-persona review of the xsmom-pit PASS and the state of the fund
**Chair's spot-checks confirmed the load-bearing claims:** ADR-0009 line 18 says "SPY total return"; report line 39 says price return on both sides; `~/AtlasBackups` does not exist and the launchd backup job is failing exactly as described.

## 1. Where the panel agrees

**The PASS does not meet your own signed bar.** Six of seven personas (all but the COO) independently found the same defect: ADR-0009 requires beating *SPY total return*, and the xsmom-pit verdict was scored price-return-vs-price-return because dividends are not ingested anywhere in the system. SPY's ~1.9%/yr yield compounds to roughly the size of the entire 153pp pass margin; the strategy's low-yield momentum tilt means the correction is asymmetric against you. Approving on this evidence would be the fund's first constitutional breach — the exact "quiet gate weakening" the house rules exist to prevent.

**Even if TR passes, the edge is thin and endpoint-concentrated.** Three personas (Quant-PM, Validation, CIO) decomposed the report's own annual table: through 2025-12-31 the cumulative edge is ~14pp over 13.5 years, ~88% of the total edge sits in 2026 H1, the strategy trails SPY in 7 of 13 full calendar years, and the biased early-membership window (339 of ~500 names) contributes materially. IR ≈ 0.2 means live P&L cannot confirm or refute this edge on any horizon you will actually run.

**The strategy you validated is not the strategy you can trade.** Four personas (Quant-PM, Trader, Risk, CIO) converged on the same arithmetic: ~50 fractional-weight names at A$100k is ~A$1,600/position — below your A$2,000 minimum; the only execution path bolts on 2×ATR stops the backtest never had; L9 smears a monthly rebalance across two weeks; L5 caps gross at 80%. The implementable variant has zero backtest evidence. Wiring the PIT recipe as-is guarantees structural live-vs-backtest divergence from day one.

**There is no un-approval path.** Four personas (Quant-PM, Risk, Validation, CIO) found that Doc 04's tolerance bands, CUSUM drift detection, and auto-demotion are signed policy with zero call sites — dead code. Once a strategy is wired, the only thing that stops it is a NAV breaker, which fires after the loss. Bands must be pre-registered *at* approval to be honest; the window to do that closes the day the gate goes green.

**The ops substrate cannot carry the soak.** Three personas (COO, CIO, Trader): production is a login item on a laptop that sleeps, zero backups exist anywhere (the launchd job has been failing silently for its entire life), the restore drill has never run, silence is indistinguishable from success, and the Phase-6-gating 60-session clock is running against an empty book on this hardware.

**Earnings gaps defeat the stop regime.** Three personas (Trader, Risk, Data): 2×ATR does not price a known binary event, the desk can propose — and you can seal — a full position the session before a print, and the calendar data is structured (dates and decimals, not injectable prose), so the deliberate news exclusion does not apply.

## 2. The ranked board list

*(ranked by impact × urgency ÷ effort; personas abbreviated Q=quant-pm, T=exec-trader, R=head-of-risk, V=validation-quant, C=fund-coo, D=data-engineer, X=cio)*

1. **Re-score xsmom-pit total-return-vs-total-return (M) — Q, T, R, V, D, X.** Problem: the PASS was scored against a benchmark the signed ADR-0009 does not permit, and the TR correction likely exceeds the pass margin. Fix: ingest EODHD dividends (new migration; corporate_actions already has the action_type), build TR series for the panel + SPY, re-run as a new registered trial, record the verdict verbatim either way.
2. **Pre-committed endpoint/subperiod kill test (S) — V, Q.** Problem: the edge is ~88% one half-year plus the biased 2012-2015 undercount window, and 2016-2025 *loses* to SPY by 14.3pp. Fix: register a kill-only start-2016 trial of the identical recipe, add verdict-vs-endpoint (rolled back monthly, 24 months) and per-fold-vs-SPY exhibits — one afternoon on warm machinery, can only demote.
3. **Off-box backup with a passed restore drill, today (S) — C.** Problem: zero dumps exist anywhere and the backup job fails silently before its own alert fires — the append-only audit chain has exactly one copy on hardware that travels. Fix: run `ops/backup.sh` by hand, restore into a scratch container, run `verify_chain`, rclone off-box, move scheduling off broken launchd; repeat monthly.
4. **Provision the Linux box; one scheduler; dead-man heartbeat (M) — C, X, T.** Problem: production was down at review time, three overlapping schedulers exist, and every alert is emitted by the process that must fail. Fix: run the already-written deploy kit (cutover doubles as restore drill #2), decommission all Mac scheduling, add a T9-completion heartbeat ping so *absence* pages.
5. **Backtest the implementable variant before any wiring ADR (L) — Q, T, R, X.** Problem: the validated construction is literally unsizable at A$100k under current policy, and the tradeable version (concentrated top-N, stops, breakers, staggered entries, real small-account costs) has never run through the gauntlet. Fix: register it as its own trial; only the overlaid configuration can be approved — and decide explicitly whether a systematic sleeve gets its own batched, human-sealed rebalance path instead of the memo bridge.
6. **Fix the stop scan to cover every unscanned bar (S) — T.** Problem: `scan_stop_exits` checks only the latest bar, so any missed cycle plus a recovering bar silently skips a stop a live broker would have filled. Fix: scan all bars since last-scanned per position, fire on the first breach at min(stop, that bar's open), emit an audit event when >1 bar is processed. Live defect in today's book.
7. **Approval contract: pre-registered bands + CUSUM auto-demote + daily shadow run (M) — Q, R, V, X.** Problem: `evaluate_approval` demands no bands, the CUSUM detector has no callers, and no shadow NAV exists — approval is currently a one-way door. Fix: refuse approval without persisted bands and CUSUM parameters, add a demoted_to_paper state, ship a daily shadow node so the 6-month evidence clock starts now.
8. **Seal the passive index core and write the allocation ADR (S) — X, Q.** Problem: 100% cash against a 50/30/20 benchmark is the fund's largest unminuted active bet, the soak is validating an empty book, and nothing binds a wired xsmom to the 40% sleeve cap (whose breaker math — 40% × −36.7% ≈ DD3 — appears nowhere). Fix: limit-set v2 row for an index-core ETF class via dual-confirm change control, seal the core through the ordinary path per ADR-0009 consequence 2, and pre-commit sleeve sizing from breaker math before any approval day.
9. **Lineage-level trial counting + pre-committed perturbation grid (M) — V, Q.** Problem: the same recipe sits in the registry as three families each at n_trials=1 (honest lineage DSR is 0.977 at n=3, 0.897 at n=10), and the single tested cell has zero perturbation analysis — the current setup rewards *not* testing. Fix: lineage column + backfill, DSR reported at lineage count, and a kill-only grid committed to the repo before any cell runs.
10. **Wire the dead Doc-04 machinery + policy-conformance test (M) — R.** Problem: stress gate, factor-overlap guard, and vol targeting are signed policy, fully coded, and have zero call sites — the first live book will be pure momentum, the exact case §12 exists for. Fix: wire them into build_proposal/approval as additional gates, then add a boundary-style test asserting every Doc-04 rule has a live call site.
11. **Earnings-calendar guard (S) — T, R, D.** Problem: the bridge can derive a 2×ATR stop the day before a known print, and one gap-through-stop wipes ~six months of expected edge at this AUM. Fix: weekly EODHD calendar fetch (structured dates, injection-safe), a recorded no-new-entry rule within N sessions of a print, and an approval-desk banner.
12. **Thirty-minute security debt + vendor tripwires (S/M) — C, X, D.** Problem: chat-exposed API keys unrotated, repo public (shipping every prompt template), zero API auth, and a single vendor whose plausible-but-wrong print flows unchecked into stops and the append-only record. Fix: rotate keys and flip the repo private now; Host/Origin allowlist on mutating endpoints; daily free-source canary on SPY/AUDUSD and stop discarding vendor adjusted_close (free per-bar corporate-action reconciliation).

## 3. The one thing

**Settle whether the fund actually has a strategy — before the approval decision, not after.** Five of seven one-things point at the same object from different angles (TR re-scoring ×3, the start-2016 kill test, the implementable-variant run): the imminent approval of xsmom-pit rests on evidence that violates the signed ADR-0009 text, concentrates its edge in one half-year plus a biased data window, and describes a portfolio the book cannot hold. Run items 1 and 2 together this month — dividends ingest plus TR re-run plus the pre-committed 2016/endpoint exhibits — and record the verdict verbatim. If it fails, you have buried your 25th trial honestly and saved months of wiring work on an artifact; if it passes, every downstream decision (implementable run, bands, allocation, wiring ADR) finally stands on compliant evidence. This is also the fund's first real test of its own constitution under the pressure the constitution was written for: the moment a PASS exists and you want it to be true.

The COO's backup finding is excluded from this competition only because it is an afternoon, not a month. Do it first, this week, regardless — a dead SSD erases the audit chain *and* the ability to run the re-test.

## 4. Dissents worth preserving

- **Sequencing (CIO vs the field).** The CIO alone insists the passive index core be sealed *this week*, independent of xsmom's fate, because the 60-session soak clock is producing evidence-free green checkmarks against an empty book. The rest of the panel implicitly sequences everything behind validation. The chair sides with the CIO on this narrow point: the core requires nothing from xsmom and ADR-0009 already sanctioned it.
- **Existential ops first (COO).** The COO would rank backup/box above all research work: every research finding is recoverable by rebuilding; the audit chain is not. Preserved because it is correct as a matter of loss ordering even though the board's one-thing went to validation.
- **The DSR regime rewards under-testing (Validation, minority).** Because every registered trial lowers deflated Sharpe, the current machinery perversely incentivizes running *fewer* robustness checks. The proposed fix — pre-registered kill-only grids where post-grid lineage DSR is binding — inverts the incentive. Structural, and nobody else saw it.
- **The economics may not close (CIO, minority).** A ~2pp/yr edge on A$100k is ~A$2k/yr against data + LLM + brokerage costs that plausibly exceed it. The roadmap itself says this criterion can kill the project. One page of arithmetic, this month, in writing.
- **Some fixes are relaxations, not upgrades (chair's note).** The L2 cap change for the index core and any L9 carve-out for batched rebalances are limit *loosenings* proposed through legitimate change control. They are probably right, but the board should name them as relaxations and sign them as such — the "gates never weaken" rule is only as strong as the honesty of its exceptions.

## 5. What the panel praised

The praise was uniform and specific: the research graveyard is real. Twenty-four recorded FAILs, a survivorship-tainted PASS the fund *demoted itself* via an ETF crosscheck it didn't have to run, FX verdicts recorded verbatim, a point-in-time panel built with 70 delisted names when the easy path was ignoring them, and an append-only audit chain wrapping all of it. Several panelists noted that their own criticisms were only findable because the reports disclose their caveats in plain text — the report that earned the PASS also supplied the ammunition to question it. That is the opposite of how most solo quant projects die, and it is why the board's recommendations are about finishing the discipline, not installing one.
